Menstrual Symptoms

Menstrual symptom data can help end period stigma. Since the topic of periods is often avoided in conversations altogether, there is a lack of understanding about when menstrual symptoms require medical attention. Menstruation has also been used as a reason to justify excluding women from health research. As a result, gender inequality has limited researchers’ understanding of menstrual, reproductive, and women’s health.

Menstrual Hygiene in different states of India.

The National Family Health Survey (NFHS)-5 data released in 2019-20 makes for a grim reading and the data on the number of women in each state who use hygienic protection methods during menstruation even more so. Here, using data from the NFHS-5, we reflect on the importance of state-level initiatives.
The state government started various schemes to improve sanitary item usage among menstruators, especially in rural areas. Some of these schemes are Beti Bachao Beti Padhao, Swachh Bharat Swachh Vidyalaya, MHM Scheme under NHM. These schemes have collectively helped in improving the sanitary item's usage. This has lead to a growth in using menstrual hygiene protections among women and girls. Still there are low percentage in states like Bihar, chattisgarh,Mandhya Pradesh, Gujrat,etc.

Girls dropping out of Schools

1 in 5 Girls Drop Out After Their Period.The stigma around menstruation has a long history of harming thousands of women, from being viewed as "untouchables" to dropping out of school, being pressured into early marriages, and more. Because of this, only 36% of people in a nation with over 355 million menstruators really utilise the sanitary napkin, a necessary component of menstrual hygiene. a lack of Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM), fueled by a lack of education, is one of the primary reasons why women in several parts of India still resort to harmful menstrual practices like using dirty rags, ash, mud, leaves and soil to manage their monthly flow.
This explains why so many girls choose to skip that particular period of the month's scheduled school days. Leading menstrual hygiene company Whisper created the #KeepGirlsInSchool campaign to raise awareness of girls leaving school after puberty in order to address this issue.

Proper Menstrual Protection

The above given pie-chart is constructed by analysis NFHS-5 data for the use of different types of Menstrual protection product by womens in India. Tampons and sanitary pads both are good methods for menstrual hygiene. Thus, having higher percentage of sanitary napkins is a good sign of improvement. What seems challange here is using higher percentage of normal cloth for menstrual protection. Unsterilised cloth and other practices makes them susceptible to urinary tract infections, fungal infection and other problems. Many women in rural areas reuse the cloth and others dry them in the shade, which is a major cause for concern.

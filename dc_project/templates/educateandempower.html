Sanitary Napkins

These are absorbent materials that catch menstrual blood when attached to the inside of girls underwear Some pads have extra material called 'wings' which helps to hold the pad in place and prevents leaking. ........ Pads should be changed every 3-4 hours, even when there is a lighter flow, this helps to prevent bacterial growth and stops odor. If the flow rate is heavier, then this period often reduces. Few of the girls have heavier blood flow while others have lighter flow during their periods. And most girls have ligher days and heavier days. Pads or sanitary napkins can vary by size or absorbency. Depending on these factors, there are different kinds of sanitary napkins: super, slender, overnight,scented,mini,maxi. So, to find a right pad, there is a need to do a little bit of experimentation.

Tempons

A tempon is made up of absorbent material compressed in a small tube. Tempons come in different sizes and absorbecies for heavier and lighter period. Some tempons come with the applicator, used to guides the tempon inside vagina. ........ While other tempons are inserted using fingers. After applying tempons into the vagina and washing your hand, make sure to follow the directions that come with the tempons carefully and relax. It is necessary to change a tempon every 4-6 hours or when it gets saturated with blood. Tempons have a string attached to its end that stays out of the girl's body. To remove the tempon, the string is gently pull on, untill the tempon comes out. If it is the time to change tempon and string can't be found, one have to reach in with their fingers. Tempons need to be change often, because if left too long for inside, it can generate infection, odor or discharge in girl's vagina.

Menstrual Cups

A menstrual cup is a funnel shaped cup made of rubber or silicon that can be inserted into the vagina to catch and collect. Unlike tempons, it is not an absorbemt material, the cup only catches the blood when it flows out of the vagina. The menstrual cup is mad ........ Menstrual cups are safer than tempons, are affordable, holds more blood than pads or tempons,, are better for environment, can be worn with UID. Depending on the rate of flow, a menstrual cup needs to be cahanged every 6-12 hours. A menstrual cup may be greased, to avoid feeling uncomfortable when applied for the first time. To apply menstrual cup, wash hands properly, apply water to the rim of the cup, tightly fold the menstrual cup in half, hoding it in a hand with rim facing up, insert the cup, rim up in vagina, once inserted, rotate it. It will spring open to create an airtight seal to stop leaks.
How to take menstrual cup out? Wash hand, place index finger and thumb into the vagina Pull the stem of the cup gently until reaching the base Pinch the base to release the seal and pull down to remove the cup.Once, its out, empty the cup into the sink.
